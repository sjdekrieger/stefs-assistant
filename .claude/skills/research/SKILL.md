# Research Skill

Deep research tailored to Stef's context: Product Design student, Amsterdam, 3D/Blender, Tenciq, freelance.

## When to use
Invoke with `/research [topic]` or when Stef asks for a deep dive into something. Not for quick factual questions — use this when the topic actually needs investigation from multiple angles.

## Research types and angles

### Design trends / Tenciq inspiration
1. What's trending in the specific niche (3D product renders, abstract visuals, etc.)
2. What top creators in this space are doing right now
3. What formats/styles are performing on Instagram in this niche
4. What's emerging — not mainstream yet but gaining traction
5. How Stef's current style/skills connect or contrast

### Freelance market research
1. What clients actually pay for 3D renders / technical drawings in the Netherlands
2. Where those clients are (platforms, industries, companies in Amsterdam)
3. What the best-positioned 3D freelancers do differently (portfolio, pricing, niche)
4. What services have the highest demand right now
5. Concrete next step: who to approach, what to offer, at what price

### Technical research (Blender, SolidWorks, Figma, etc.)
1. Best current method to achieve the specific result
2. Common mistakes or pitfalls for this technique
3. Resources: specific tutorials, docs, community threads
4. How long it realistically takes to learn/implement
5. Whether there's a faster/smarter approach Stef hasn't considered

### Inspiration research (style, aesthetic, creator deep dive)
1. Background and context on the subject
2. What makes it work visually/conceptually
3. Techniques and tools behind it
4. How to apply or adapt it to Stef's work
5. Similar references he should also look at

## Process

**Step 1 — Clarify**
Before searching, identify:
- What type of research is this?
- What's the actual end goal? (Tenciq post, portfolio piece, freelance pitch, skill improvement, something else)
- What does Stef already know / what's the gap?

**Step 2 — Multi-angle search**
Run at minimum 3 searches, never just one. Each from a different angle:
- The direct question
- The practitioner angle ("what do working 3D designers say about X")
- The Amsterdam / Dutch market angle where relevant
- The current/trending angle (add "2025" or "2026" to queries)
- The contrarian angle ("why X doesn't work" or "alternatives to X")

**Step 3 — Filter through Stef's context**
Before synthesizing, ask:
- Is this realistic for a design student with limited time and budget?
- Does this connect to his current priorities (portfolio, Tenciq, €1000 freelance goal)?
- Is this relevant to his tools (Blender, SolidWorks, Figma)?
- Is the Amsterdam/Dutch market context relevant here?

**Step 4 — Synthesize**
Never dump links. Deliver:
- **3–5 key insights** — the actual takeaways, not summaries of sources
- **What this means for Stef specifically** — connect it to his situation
- **1–3 concrete next actions** — specific, not vague ("post a render of X this week" not "post more content")
- **What to ignore** — explicitly call out what's not relevant for him

## Output format
- Lead with the most surprising or useful insight
- Bullet points for scanability
- Bold the key takeaways
- End with next actions, ranked by impact
- Keep it tight — if it's getting long, cut the least important 30%

## Connecting back to priorities
Always end by noting if the research connects to:
- Building the portfolio
- Growing Tenciq
- Landing first freelance income
- Finishing school on time
- Any current deadline or project
